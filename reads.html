Journalist John Naughton writes in this piece that, “the new masters of our universe are people who are essentially only half-educated.” If they had been fully educated, they may have had more foresight about the problems that could arise from their technologies. As someone pursuing a STEM-focused undergraduate education, I recognize that one of my limitations is that I will not be able to engage with many humanities, art, and "real world knowledge" courses. I've recently rekindled my interest in reading, and some of my favorites are listed below.

  • Meditations by Marcus Aurelius

    personal thoughts and affirmations of one of the greatest leaders in history

  • the five people you meet in heaven by Mitch Albom

    my favorite novel, which may or may not put you in an existential crisis

  • Educated: A Memoir by Tara Westover

    Tara has lived an unfathomable life

  • The Autobiography of Malcolm X by Malcolm X and Alex Haley

    insight into the life of man whose ideas were beyond his time

  • The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger

    a self-searching protagonist who is equal parts relatable and unrelatable

  • Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki

    a conceptual personal finance book that I plan to re-read many more times

  • Mamba Mentality by by Kobe Bryant

    the late, great Kobe Bryant imparts anecdotes on his obsession with being the best version himself

  • So You Want to Talk About Race by Iejoma Oluo

    a relevant, digestable piece on racial injustice and systemic racism

  • The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini

    a story of culture, relationships, and loyalty
